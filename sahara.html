The Sahara Desert

The Sahara Desert is a vast, arid region that covers much of North Africa, including parts of Tunisia. In fact, the southern part of Tunisia is almost entirely covered by the Sahara, making it a popular destination for travelers seeking to explore this unique and beautiful landscape.

Despite its reputation as a barren and inhospitable environment, the Sahara in Tunisia is home to a surprising amount of life. A variety of plant and animal species have adapted to the harsh conditions, including hardy desert shrubs, cacti, and even small rodents and reptiles


One of the most popular ways to explore the Sahara in Tunisia is by taking a guided camel trek through the desert. This allows visitors to experience the vastness and solitude of the desert, as well as its incredible natural beauty. Along the way, travelers may also encounter nomadic Bedouin tribes, who have lived in the Sahara for centuries and still maintain a traditional way of life.
